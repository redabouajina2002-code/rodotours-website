Where to Go

Morocco's Most Iconic Destinations

From imperial cities to desert dunes — each destination tells a unique story of Morocco's rich history and breathtaking nature.

Marrakech

🌹 The Red City

Morocco's most vibrant city — a sensory explosion of colors, sounds, and aromas. Lose yourself in the ancient medina's labyrinthine souks, marvel at the Koutoubia Mosque, and experience the legendary energy of Jemaa el-Fna square at sunset.

Direct flights from Europe
Best: Oct – Apr
Min. 2–3 days
4.9/5 rating
🕌 Medina 🛍️ Souks 🌿 Gardens 🍽️ Food Scene 🏰 Palaces
Best Time to VisitOctober to April

Merzouga

🏜️ Gateway to the Sahara

Home to the magnificent Erg Chebbi dunes — some of Morocco's highest at over 150 meters. Experience the magic of a camel trek at sunset, sleep under a billion stars in a traditional desert camp, and wake up to the most breathtaking sunrise you'll ever witness.

9 hours from Marrakech
Best: Oct – Mar
Min. 1–2 nights
5.0/5 rating
🐪 Camel Trek ⭐ Stargazing 🏕️ Desert Camp 🌅 Sunrise
Best Time to VisitOctober to March
See Tours

Fes

🏛️ Imperial City

Morocco's cultural and spiritual capital — home to the world's oldest university and one of the most complete medieval cities on earth. The Fes el-Bali medina is a UNESCO World Heritage site where time seems to have stood still for centuries.

International airport
Best: Mar – May, Sep – Nov
Min. 2–3 days
4.8/5 rating
🎓 Al-Qarawiyyin 🏺 Tanneries 🕌 Madrasas 🎨 Crafts
Best Time to VisitSpring & Autumn

Chefchaouen

🔵 The Blue Pearl

Nestled in the Rif Mountains, Chefchaouen is Morocco's most photographed city — famous for its dreamy blue-painted walls and streets. Founded in 1471, this charming mountain town offers a magical, peaceful atmosphere unlike anywhere else in Morocco.

600m altitude
Best: Apr – Jun, Sep – Oct
Min. 1–2 days
4.9/5 rating
📸 Photography 🏔️ Hiking 🛍️ Crafts 💧 Waterfalls
Best Time to VisitApril to October

Aït Ben Haddou

🏯 UNESCO World Heritage

One of Morocco's most spectacular sights — a fortified ancient city rising dramatically from the banks of the Ounila River. This UNESCO World Heritage Site has served as the backdrop for dozens of Hollywood films including Gladiator and Game of Thrones.

3.5h from Marrakech
Best: Oct – Apr
Half to full day
4.9/5 rating
🎬 Film Location 🏛️ UNESCO 📸 Photography 🏺 History
Best Time to VisitOctober to April

Essaouira

🌊 The Windy City

A UNESCO-listed coastal gem on Morocco's Atlantic coast — famous for its whitewashed medina, vibrant blue fishing boats, fresh seafood, and world-class windsurfing. Essaouira's laid-back bohemian atmosphere makes it the perfect escape from the bustle of the imperial cities.

2.5h from Marrakech
Best: May – Oct
Min. 1–2 days
4.8/5 rating
