Best Time to Visit Morocco: A Complete Month-by-Month Guide

Morocco is a year-round destination — but the timing of your visit makes a huge difference. Here's our local expert guide to every month of the year.

July 2025 8 min read Beldia Tours Team

One of the most common questions we get from travelers planning their Morocco trip is: "When is the best time to visit?" The honest answer is that it depends on what you want to experience — the Sahara desert, the imperial cities, or the Atlantic coast all have their ideal seasons.

As local experts based in Marrakech, we've guided thousands of travelers across all seasons. Here's our definitive, honest guide.

🌸 Spring (March – May) — The Best Overall Season

Spring is widely considered the best time to visit Morocco. The weather is warm but not hot — typically 20-25°C in Marrakech and 15-20°C in the mountains. The landscapes are green, wildflowers bloom in the valleys, and the famous rose harvest in the Dadès Valley happens in May.

  • Perfect temperatures for sightseeing in cities and medinas
  • Ideal for Atlas Mountains trekking
  • Rose Festival in Kelaat M'Gouna (May)
  • Comfortable for Sahara desert trips
  • Moderate tourist crowds
💡 Beldia Tours Tip

Book your spring tour at least 6-8 weeks in advance — it's our busiest season and the best riads fill up fast!

☀️ Summer (June – August) — Hot But Possible

Summer in Morocco means intense heat, especially in the south. Marrakech regularly reaches 38-42°C in July and August. The Sahara desert can exceed 45°C during the day. However, coastal cities like Essaouira and Agadir are cooled by Atlantic breezes and are very pleasant.

  • Very hot inland — not recommended for Sahara tours
  • Perfect for Atlantic coast (Essaouira, Agadir, Tangier)
  • Fewer tourists in desert areas
  • Chefchaouen is cool and beautiful
  • Lower prices at some hotels

🍂 Autumn (September – November) — Our Second Favourite

Autumn rivals spring as the best time to visit. September and October see temperatures dropping to a comfortable 25-30°C. The summer crowds have gone, prices drop, and the light for photography is spectacular. The Sahara is warm but manageable.

  • Excellent temperatures for all activities
  • Perfect Sahara desert conditions
  • Beautiful light for photography
  • Fewer tourists than spring
  • Date harvest season in the south (October)

❄️ Winter (December – February) — Hidden Gem Season

Winter is Morocco's best-kept secret. While it can be cold in the mountains and at night, daytime temperatures in Marrakech are a lovely 18-22°C — perfect for sightseeing. The Sahara desert at night in winter is magical — cold and clear with incredible stargazing. Snow on the Atlas Mountains creates stunning landscapes.

  • Lowest prices of the year
  • Almost no tourist crowds
  • Magical stargazing in the Sahara
  • Snow on Atlas Mountains (great for photography)
  • Perfect for city tours and medina exploration
💡 Beldia Tours Tip

December and January are our favourite months for Sahara tours. Cold nights mean incredible stars and you have the dunes almost to yourself at sunrise. Bring a warm jacket!

The Complete Sahara Desert Guide: Everything You Need to Know Before You Go

The Sahara is the highlight of almost every Morocco trip. Here's our insider guide to make sure your desert experience is perfect.

June 2025 10 min read Aziz, Senior Guide

The Sahara desert is one of the world's most extraordinary places — and Merzouga's Erg Chebbi dunes are Morocco's most spectacular stretch of golden sand. After guiding hundreds of travelers through the desert, our senior guide Aziz shares everything you need to know.

🗺️ How to Get to the Sahara from Marrakech

Merzouga, the gateway to Erg Chebbi, is approximately 560km from Marrakech — about 9-10 hours by road. The journey itself is spectacular, passing through the High Atlas Mountains, the UNESCO-listed Aït Ben Haddou kasbah, the Draa Valley, and Todra Gorge.

We recommend a minimum 3-day tour from Marrakech — this gives you one night in the desert and enough time to experience the journey properly. A 4-day tour allows for more stops and a second night in the desert.

💡 Beldia Tours Tip

Never rush the Marrakech to Merzouga journey. The stops along the way — Aït Ben Haddou, Todra Gorge, the Valley of Roses — are as memorable as the desert itself.

🐪 The Camel Trek Experience

The traditional way to reach your desert camp is by camel — a 45-60 minute ride through the golden dunes as the sun sets. It's one of the most magical experiences Morocco offers. Here's what to expect:

  • Camels are well-trained and gentle — no experience needed
  • Your guide leads the camel — you just enjoy the view
  • It can be bumpy — hold on and go with the movement
  • Bring a headscarf or turban to protect from sand and sun
  • The return ride at sunrise is equally magical

⭐ Desert Camp — What to Expect

Desert camps range from basic (shared tents, simple meals) to luxury glamping (private ensuite tents, gourmet food, hot showers). We work with carefully selected camps that offer the best experience.

  • Traditional Berber dinner under the stars
  • Live music around the campfire
  • Incredible stargazing — the Sahara has zero light pollution
  • Wake up call before sunrise for the most beautiful light
  • Hot mint tea served at the top of the dunes

🎒 What to Pack for the Sahara

  • Headscarf or turban — essential protection from sun and sand
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ — the desert sun is intense
  • Sunglasses — protecting your eyes from sand
  • Closed shoes — sandals let sand in; trainers are better
  • Warm layers — desert nights are cold even in summer
  • Camera/phone charger — sunrises here are unforgettable
  • Cash — for tips and small purchases in villages
💡 Beldia Tours Tip

The best photos happen at golden hour — 30 minutes before sunset and 30 minutes after sunrise. Position yourself at the top of a dune facing away from the sun for the most dramatic shots.

🌡️ Desert Temperatures by Season

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 40-48°C daytime, 25°C night — very hot, not recommended
  • Spring/Autumn: 25-35°C daytime, 15°C night — perfect
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 18-25°C daytime, 5°C night — magical stargazing, bring warm clothes

Marrakech Travel Guide: The 15 Best Things to Do in the Red City

Marrakech is one of the world's most vibrant cities. Here are the 15 experiences you absolutely cannot miss — from local experts who live here.

May 2025 9 min read Beldia Tours Team

Marrakech — the Red City — is Morocco's most visited destination and for good reason. It's a city that assaults your senses in the most wonderful way: the smell of spices, the sound of musicians in Jemaa el-Fna, the maze of the ancient medina. Here are the 15 things you must do.

1. 🌟 Jemaa el-Fna Square — The Heart of Marrakech

UNESCO-listed as a Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage, Jemaa el-Fna is unlike any other square on earth. By day it hosts snake charmers, henna artists, and orange juice sellers. By night it transforms into a massive outdoor food festival with dozens of food stalls, musicians, storytellers, and acrobats.

💡 Local Tip

Arrive at the square just before sunset and find a rooftop cafe. Watch the square transform as night falls — it's one of the most spectacular sights in Africa.

2. 🛍️ The Souks — Get Lost on Purpose

The medina's souks are organized by trade — spice souks, leather souks, textile souks, metalwork souks. Getting lost is part of the experience. Don't be afraid to wander — you'll always find your way back to Jemaa el-Fna eventually.

3. 🏰 Bahia Palace

Built in the late 19th century, Bahia Palace is a masterpiece of Moroccan architecture — intricate tilework, carved cedar ceilings, and peaceful orange-tree gardens. Allow 45-60 minutes to explore.

4. 🌿 Majorelle Garden & Yves Saint Laurent Museum

Created by French painter Jacques Majorelle and later restored by Yves Saint Laurent, this stunning garden features cobalt blue buildings, exotic plants, and the excellent Berber Museum. Book tickets in advance — it sells out daily.

5. 🕌 Koutoubia Mosque

The 12th-century Koutoubia Mosque is Marrakech's most iconic landmark — its 70-metre minaret visible from almost everywhere in the city. Non-Muslims cannot enter but the gardens and exterior are beautiful, especially at sunset.

6. 🏺 Saadian Tombs

Discovered only in 1917 after being sealed for two centuries, the Saadian Tombs contain the remains of members of the Saadian dynasty. The intricate tilework and carved plaster are extraordinary.

7. 🍽️ Eat at a Medina Restaurant

Marrakech's food scene is incredible. Don't miss: tagine with preserved lemon and olives, bastilla (pigeon or chicken pastry), harira soup, fresh-squeezed orange juice from the square, and Moroccan mint tea.

💡 Local Tip

Avoid restaurants directly on Jemaa el-Fna — they're overpriced and average quality. Walk 2-3 streets into the medina for much better food at half the price.

8. 🛁 Visit a Traditional Hammam

A Moroccan hammam (steam bath) is a cultural experience not to be missed. The traditional black soap scrub leaves your skin incredibly smooth. Ask your riad or guide to recommend a good local hammam — not the tourist ones.

9. 🌅 Day Trip to Atlas Mountains

Just 1 hour from Marrakech, the High Atlas Mountains offer a completely different Morocco — Berber villages, walnut groves, and snow-capped peaks. A day trip or overnight stay is highly recommended.

10. 🏰 Day Trip to Aït Ben Haddou

Morocco's most famous kasbah — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and filming location for Gladiator, Game of Thrones, and dozens of other productions. 3.5 hours from Marrakech but absolutely worth it.

11-15. More Hidden Gems

  • El Badi Palace ruins — hauntingly beautiful 16th-century palace
  • Ben Youssef Madrasa — stunning Islamic school with intricate architecture
  • Mellah (Jewish Quarter) — fascinating history and beautiful synagogue
  • Tanneries of the Medina — watch traditional leather dyeing from rooftop terraces
  • Agafay Desert Day Trip — rocky desert just 40 minutes from Marrakech, perfect for sunset dinners
