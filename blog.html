Best Time to Visit Morocco: A Month-by-Month Guide

Morocco is a year-round destination — but the timing makes a huge difference. Spring and autumn are best for the Sahara, summer for the coast, winter for stargazing.

July 2025 8 min read

🌸 Spring (March – May) — The Best Overall Season

Spring is the best time to visit Morocco. Temperatures are perfect at 20-25°C, landscapes are green, and the famous rose harvest happens in May. Ideal for both city tours and Sahara desert trips.

☀️ Summer (June – August) — Hot But Possible

Very hot inland (38-42°C in Marrakech). Best for coastal cities like Essaouira. Not recommended for Sahara tours due to extreme heat.

🍂 Autumn (September – November) — Our Second Favourite

Excellent temperatures (25-30°C), fewer tourists, and perfect Sahara conditions. Great photography light and date harvest season in October.

❄️ Winter (December – February) — Hidden Gem Season

Lowest prices, no crowds, magical Sahara stargazing. Daytime in Marrakech is pleasant at 18-22°C. Snow on Atlas Mountains creates stunning scenery.

💡 Beldia Tours Tip

December and January are magical for Sahara tours — cold nights mean incredible stars and you have the dunes almost to yourself at sunrise!

The Complete Sahara Desert Guide: Everything You Need to Know

Our senior guide Aziz shares everything you need to know before your Sahara adventure — from what to pack to the best camps.

June 2025 10 min read

🗺️ How to Get to the Sahara from Marrakech

Merzouga is about 560km from Marrakech — a 9-10 hour drive. The journey passes through the Atlas Mountains, Aït Ben Haddou, Draa Valley, and Todra Gorge. We recommend minimum 3 days from Marrakech.

🐪 The Camel Trek Experience

A 45-60 minute camel ride to your desert camp as the sun sets. Camels are gentle and well-trained. Bring a headscarf for protection from sand and sun. The return ride at sunrise is equally magical.

🎒 What to Pack for the Sahara

Headscarf, sunscreen SPF50+, sunglasses, closed shoes, warm layers (desert nights are cold), camera, and cash for tips. Most importantly — bring your sense of wonder!

💡 Beldia Tours Tip

The best photos happen at golden hour — 30 minutes before sunset. Position yourself at the top of a dune facing away from the sun for dramatic shots!

Marrakech Travel Guide: The 15 Best Things to Do in the Red City

Marrakech assaults your senses in the most wonderful way. Here are the 15 things you absolutely cannot miss — from local experts who live here.

May 2025 9 min read
1. Jemaa el-Fna Square

UNESCO-listed — snake charmers by day, food festival by night. Watch the sunset from a rooftop café.

2. The Souks

Organized by trade — spice, leather, textile, metalwork. Getting lost is part of the experience!

3. Bahia Palace

Masterpiece of Moroccan architecture with intricate tilework and peaceful orange-tree gardens.

4. Majorelle Garden

Created by Yves Saint Laurent. Stunning cobalt blue buildings and exotic plants. Book in advance!

5. Koutoubia Mosque

12th century iconic landmark — 70m minaret visible from everywhere. Beautiful at sunset.

6. Traditional Hammam

Moroccan steam bath with black soap scrub. A cultural experience not to be missed!

7. Atlas Mountains Day Trip

1 hour from Marrakech — Berber villages, walnut groves, snow-capped peaks.

8. Aït Ben Haddou

UNESCO site and filming location for Gladiator & Game of Thrones. 3.5h from Marrakech.

💡 Local Tip

Avoid restaurants directly on Jemaa el-Fna — they are overpriced. Walk 2-3 streets into the medina for much better food at half the price!
